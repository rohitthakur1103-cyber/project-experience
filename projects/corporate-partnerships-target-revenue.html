Tableau case study

Corporate Partnerships Target Revenue

This project uses Tableau to model a multi-year partnership revenue path and show how the target can be distributed across sponsorship asset categories. The workbook is designed as a planning tool rather than a static report. The original workbook came from an older corporate Tableau environment, so this page keeps the project reviewable without relying on a live connection.

  • Tableau
  • Revenue planning
  • Scenario modeling
  • Commercial analytics

Core idea

Partnership targets are easier to defend when the growth path and asset mix are visible in one place. This workbook turns the planning logic into an adjustable model so revenue assumptions can be reviewed instead of recalculated by hand.

Default model inputs

  • First year: 2026
  • Planning horizon: 4 years
  • Annual growth rate: 6%
  • First-year target: $100,000

What the workbook calculates

  • Year-by-year target growth from a selected starting point.
  • Dynamic allocation of the target across sponsorship asset categories.
  • Category-level revenue values tied directly to the chosen asset mix.
  • A cleaner scenario-planning view for commercial review.

Why this matters

The value here is not just the workbook itself. It shows the ability to take spreadsheet-driven business logic, structure it inside Tableau, and turn it into something that can support revenue conversations, packaging decisions, and executive review.
